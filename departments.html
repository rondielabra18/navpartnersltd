Industrial Manufacturer

Operating machinery, assembling products, and conducting quality checks in a fast-paced environment to meet production goals. Key responsibilities include, maintaining equipment, following safety protocols, and documenting work, with common roles including assembly workers, technicians, and machine operators. These positions require technical skills, attention to detail, and physical stamina for tasks like lifting and standing.

Key Responsibilites

Production Operation

Operating, monitoring, and adjusting industrial machinery and conveyor lines.

Assembly & Fabrication

Assembling components, parts, or products on a production line according to specifications.

Quality Control

Inspecting raw materials and finished goods for defects and compliance with standards.

Team Collaboration

Working with team members and supervisors to meet daily production quotas and solve issues.

Nursing Care/Services

Providing direct patient care, assessing health needs, developing, implementing, and evaluating treatment plans, and monitoring patient progress. Nurses administer medications, perform diagnostic tests, maintain medical records, and collaborate with physicians to promote recovery while offering emotional support to patients and families.

Key Responsibilities

Patient Assessment & Care

Perform physical exams, take vital signs (pulses, temperatures, blood pressure), and monitor conditions to evaluate needs.

Treatment Administration

Administer medications, change wound dressings, and operate medical equipment.

Care Planning

Develop, implement, and update individualized patient care plans.

Collaboration & Communication

Work with doctors and multidisciplinary teams, report changes in patient conditions, and educate patients and families on disease prevention and treatment.

Agriculturist

Handles comprehensive crop cultivation or livestock management, including planting, harvesting, sorting, and shipping produce or tending to animals. They work in greenhouses, farms, or nurseries, operating machinery and ensuring quality control for agricultural products.

Key Responsibilities

Cultivation Management

Planting seeds/seedlings, watering, fertilizing, and managing crop growth.

Harvesting and Post-Harvest

Harvesting, sorting, cleaning, packaging, and shipping products (e.g., lettuce, cabbage, tomatoes).

Equipment Operation

Using and maintaining farm machinery like tractors, sprayers, and greenhouses systems.

Livestock Management

Feeding, breeding, and managing health for pigs, cattle, or chickens (if in livestock farming).

Shipbuilding

Construct, assemble, and repair ships by welding, fitting, painting, and installing machinery or electrical systems. Under the Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) program, workers follow supervisor instructions, read blueprints, and use tools to align steel components.

Key Responsibilities

Welder/Fabricator

Specializes in structural welding, joining steel plates, and assembling ship components.

Steel Processing/Shipfitter

Reads blueprints and sketches to cut, bend, and align steel parts for hull structures.

Machinery & Electrical Installation

Installs piping, engines, and electrical/electronic equipment.

Inspection & Supervision

Oversees ship assembly, conducts sea trials, and ensures quality standards.

Construction Worker

Perform, supervise, or assist in building, renovating, and maintaining structures (residential, commercial, or public infrastructure). Key tasks include operating machinery (cranes, excavators), carpentry, masonry, painting, scaffolding, and site preparation, with an intense focus on strict safety protocols, team collaboration, and daily site cleanup.

Key Responsibilities

Key Roles

Typical roles include general laborers, carpenters, steel fixers, scaffolders, and heavy machine operators.

Skills Needed

Reading blueprints, assembling structures, pouring concrete, installing pipes/electrical systems, and maintaining tools.

Work Environment & Culture

Strict adherence to safety (wearing PPE is mandatory), starting the day with radio taiso (warm-up exercises), and respecting strict hierarchical, team-based, and senior-junior relationships.

Requirements

Physical fitness and strength, ability to work outdoors in varying weather, and basic Japanese communication skills are generally required.
